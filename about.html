About Paul Graham

Paul Graham is a programmer, venture capitalist, and essayist. He is known for his work on Lisp, for co-founding Viaweb (which eventually became Yahoo! Store), and for co-founding the Y Combinator seed capital firm.

Graham is the author of several programming books, such as: On Lisp (1993), ANSI Common Lisp (1995), and Hackers & Painters (2004). He is also known for creating the Bayesian spam filter.

In addition to his technical work, Graham is known for his essays on entrepreneurship, programming, and life philosophy. His essays are widely read in the tech and startup communities and have influenced many entrepreneurs and programmers.

In 2005, Graham co-founded Y Combinator with Jessica Livingston, Robert Morris, and Trevor Blackwell. Y Combinator was the first of a new type of startup incubator and has funded over 2,000 startups, including Stripe, Airbnb, Dropbox, Coinbase, and Reddit. About This Website

This website is a collection of essays by Paul Graham. It provides a clean, modern, and accessible way to read Paul Graham's essays and download them in EPUB format for offline reading.

The website is automatically updated every week to include new essays as they are published on Paul Graham's website, ensuring you always have access to his latest thoughts and insights.

About the Developer

This website was created by Emmanuel Humbling, a developer who has been deeply influenced by Paul Graham's essays and the impact of his work.

I've long admired Paul Graham not just for his technical insights, but for his ability to articulate complex ideas about startups, technology, and life in a clear and compelling way. What Paul and his friends have accomplished over the years through Y Combinator has transformed the startup ecosystem and created a platform for thousands of founders to bring their ideas to life.

His essays have shaped my thinking about technology, entrepreneurship, and problem-solving. This website is my small contribution to help make these valuable writings more accessible to everyone.
